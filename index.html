All Against Odds

Trevor’s life journey proves that no matter how hard your start is, you can rise.he faced endless challenges but turned his story into inspiration. Being born mixed-race during apartheid was considered illegal and dangerous. Trevor’s life was filled with poverty, racism, violence and a divided society. Despite all of that,he found strength through his intelligence, humor, and the unconditional love of his mother. The title Against All Odds captures the resilience, determination and courage it took for him to survive and eventually succeed. It honors his story as one of mastery in the face of extreme adversity.

“We tell people to follow their dreams, but you can only dream of what you can imagine—and, depending on where you come from, your imagination can be quite limited.”

Born a Crime

Born a Crime is a powerful memoir by comedian Trevor Noah, sharing his story of growing up in apartheid and post-apartheid South Africa. Born to a Black mother and a White father,Trevor's existence was literally a crime under South African law.

Born a Crime: Personal Reflection

"Abel wanted a traditional marriage with a traditional wife… but he never fell in love with subservient women… 'He only wants a woman who is free because his dream is to put her in a cage."

This quote made me think about how some people admire independence women in idea but feel insecure by them in practice. This thinking still exist in today’s society.Many women are encouraged to be outspoken and confident publicly. However, in private relationships,those same charachters can be seen as "too much" or "intimidating" by men who expect more traditional and submissive roles. This contradiction mirrors Abel’s mindset, loving the idea of a free and strong woman, while craving dominance over his wife. Today, while gender roles are changing, the old expectations are still much alive. Men have the perception that women have to be controlled by their husbands. Social media makes this pressure even worse. On platforms like Instagram and TikTok, there’s a lot of talk about "soft life" and "submissive girlfriend" trends, where women are encouraged to be dependent and gentle to attract men. This book make me realize that real love should not feel like losing pieces of yourself to make someone else comfortable. Love should be a space where both partners grow, thrive and support each other’s freedom and choices.Trevor’s story teaches us relationships built on respect are the ones that last, while those built on control and fear only cause pain to both partners. Patricia’s courage reminds us that staying true to yourself is more important than fitting into someone else’s idea of who you should be. It shows how dangerous it is when men want ownership instead of partnership in marriage.In today’s world, we must continue to challenge old expectations and create relationships where freedom and love go hand in hand, women's voices are heard and their opinion matters.
