BOOK REVIEW BLOG

All Against Odds

Trevor’s life proves that no matter how hard your start is, you can rise.he faced endless challenges but turned his story into inspiration. It reminds us resilience beats circumstance.It reflects Trevor Noah’s journey of overcoming nearly impossible challenges and obstacles. Being born mixed-race during apartheid was considered illegal and dangerous. Trevor’s life was filled with poverty, racism, violence and a divided society. Despite all of that,he found strength through his intelligence, humor, and the unconditional love of his mother. The title Against All Odds captures the resilience, determination and courage it took for him to survive and eventually succeed. It honors his story as one of mastery in the face of extreme adversity.

“We tell people to follow their dreams, but you can only dream of what you can imagine—and, depending on where you come from, your imagination can be quite limited.”

Born a Crime

Born a Crime is a powerful memoir by comedian Trevor Noah, sharing his story of growing up in apartheid and post-apartheid South Africa. Born to a Black mother and a White father,Trevor's existence was literally a crime under South African law.

Born a Crime: Personal Reflection

"Abel wanted a traditional marriage with a traditional wife… but he never fell in love with subservient women… 'He only wants a woman who is free because his dream is to put her in a cage."

This quote made me think about how some people admire independence in theory but feel threatened by itin practice. It is a powerful reminder that true love should never feel like a cage and freedom within love should be protected not taken away.This dynamic still resonates in today’s society.Many women are encouraged to be outspoken, confident publicly. However, in private relationships,those same traits can be seen as "too much" or "intimidating" by partners who expect more traditional, submissive roles. The idea of a “strong woman” is celebrated, but only up to the point where it doesn’t challenge the man's ego or authority. This contradiction mirrors Abel’s mindset, loving the idea of a free woman, while craving dominance, control and submission.Today, while gender roles are changing, the old expectations still linger.Women are judged for not being "traditional enough" at home. Social media makes this pressure even worse. On platforms like Instagram or TikTok, there’s a lot of talk about "soft life" and "submissive girlfriend" trends, where women are encouraged to be dependent and gentle to attract men. It shows that old ideas about control and gender roles are still very much alive.This book make me realize that real love should not feel like losing pieces of yourself to make someone else comfortable. Love should be a space where both people grow, thrive and support each other’s freedom and choices.Trevor’s story teaches us relationships built on respect are the ones that last, while those built on control and fear only cause pain. Patricia’s courage reminds us that staying true to yourself is more important than fitting into someone else’s idea of who you should be. Patricia’s bravery show how dangerous and painful it is when love becomes about ownership instead of partnership. In today’s world, we must continue to challenge old expectations and create relationships where freedom and love go hand in hand.
